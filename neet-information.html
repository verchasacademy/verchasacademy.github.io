NEET (UG)- EXAM OVERVIEW

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is a national-level medical entrance examination conducted for admission to MBBS, BDS, and other undergraduate medical courses in India. NEET is administered by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and serves as the gateway for aspiring doctors and dentists to secure admission to both government and private medical institutions across the country.

NEET Exam Eligibility Criteria

Criteria For NEET Examination
Nationality Indian Nationals, NRIs, OCIs, PIOs, and Foreign Nationals are eligible to apply.
Age Limit Candidates must be at least 17 years old by December 31, 2025. There is no upper age limit as per the Supreme Court's order.
Educational Qualification Must have passed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.
Minimum Marks in Qualifying Exam General category: 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology
OBC/SC/ST: 40% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology
PwD: 45% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology
Number of Attempts There is no limit on the number of attempts.
Year of Passing Candidates appearing for their qualifying exam in 2026 are also eligible to apply.

NEET Exam Pattern

The NEET exam pattern is designed to test candidates' knowledge and understanding across Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.

NEET Paper Pattern
Mode of Exam Pen & Paper
Duration 3 Hours
Total Questions 180 Questions
Sectional Distribution Physics: 45 Questions
Chemistry: 45 Questions
Biology: 90 Questions
Marking Scheme +4 for every correct answer
-1 for every incorrect answer
0 marks for unattempted questions
Maximum marks 720 Marks
Question Type Multiple-choice questions (MCQs) with four options for each question
Language Options NEET is conducted in multiple languages, including English, Hindi, Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu, and Urdu.
