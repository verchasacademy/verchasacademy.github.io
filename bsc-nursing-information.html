BSc Nursing Entrance Exams - OVERVIEW

BSc Nursing is a professional undergraduate nursing programme for students who want to build a career in healthcare, hospitals, clinical care and allied medical services. Admission routes may vary by institution and state. Important pathways include AIIMS BSc Nursing entrance examination, NEET UG based admission for selected nursing programmes, and state/university-level nursing entrance tests.

General Eligibility Criteria

Common Eligibility For BSc Nursing Admission
Educational QualificationClass 12/equivalent with science subjects. Most nursing entrance pathways require Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English.
Minimum MarksMinimum qualifying marks vary by institute, category and examination authority. Students should check the latest official prospectus for the exam they are applying for.
Age CriteriaAge rules vary by exam and institution. Candidates must follow the specific eligibility criteria issued by the concerned admission authority.
Admission RouteAdmission may be through AIIMS entrance, NEET UG based counselling, state nursing entrance tests or university-level admission processes.

Exam Pattern and Preparation Areas

BSc Nursing Entrance Preparation Pattern
Major SubjectsPhysics, Chemistry, Biology and English; some exams may also include aptitude or general knowledge.
Question TypeUsually multiple-choice questions, though the exact structure depends on the exam authority.
Core FocusNCERT-based Biology, basic Physics and Chemistry, English comprehension, speed, accuracy and exam temperament.
Test PracticeTopic-wise tests, mixed practice papers, full-length mock tests and performance analysis are important for improvement.
