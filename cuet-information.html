CUET (UG) - EXAM OVERVIEW

The Common University Entrance Test CUET (UG) is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to undergraduate programmes in Central Universities and other participating universities, including state, deemed and private universities across India.

CUET UG Eligibility Criteria

Criteria For CUET UG Examination
Conducting BodyNational Testing Agency (NTA)
PurposeAdmission to undergraduate programmes in Central Universities and other participating universities.
Educational QualificationCandidates should have passed or be appearing in Class 12/equivalent examination. Final admission eligibility is decided by the university and programme chosen by the candidate.
Age LimitCUET UG itself generally does not prescribe a common age limit; candidates must satisfy the age criteria, if any, of the university where admission is sought.
Subject ChoiceStudents choose papers based on the eligibility requirements of the universities and courses they are targeting.

CUET UG Exam Pattern

CUET UG Pattern
Mode of ExamComputer Based Test (CBT)
Question TypeMultiple-choice questions (MCQs)
Major AreasLanguage papers, domain-specific subjects and general test, depending on the candidate's selected papers.
Score UseParticipating universities use CUET scores along with their own admission rules, programme requirements, cut-offs and counselling process.
Preparation FocusNCERT-based subject command, reading comprehension, reasoning, quantitative aptitude, time management and mock-test analysis.
